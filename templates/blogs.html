Tata Power EV Charging Solutions Limited and Indian Oil Corporation Limited (IOCL) have sign MoU for setting up fast and ultra-fast electric vehicle (EV) charging stations across India.
12 Dec 2023 12:40 A.M. IST

Tata Power EV Charging Solutions Limited (TPEVCSL), part of the Tata Power Group and a leading provider of EV Charging solutions, along with Indian Oil Corporation Limited (IOCL), have agreed through a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) to establish fast and ultra-fast electric vehicle (EV) charging stations across India. The partnership aims to create a widespread and dependable intercity charging infrastructure.
This initiative will see the installation of over 500 EV charging points at various IOCL retail outlets, targeting major cities such as Mumbai, Delhi, Kolkata, Bengaluru, Ahmedabad, Pune, and Kochi, and key highways including the Mumbai-Pune Expressway, Salem-Kochi Highway, Guntur-Chennai Highway, and the Golden Quadrilateral. This strategic alliance aims to build a vast and reliable intercity charging network, mitigating range anxiety for EV drivers on intercity routes.

Tata Motors to share JLR’s ‘EMA’ EV architecture for Avinya range of cars
03 Nov 2023 10:00 A.M. IST

PEML and JLR, both of which are Tata Motors subsidiaries, have entered into a Memorandum of Understanding for the licensing of EMA for upcoming Tata EVs.
Tata Passenger Electric Mobility Ltd (TPEM) and Jaguar Land Rover Plc (JLR), both 100 percent subsidiaries of Tata Motors Limited (TML), have entered into a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) for the licensing of JLR’s Electrified Modular Architecture (EMA) platform for a royalty fee (including electrical architecture, electric drive unit, battery pack and manufacturing know-hows) for the development of TPEM’s ‘premium pure electric’ vehicles series ‘Avinya’ on the EMA platform. TPEM and JLR will also enter into an Engineering Services Agreement (ESA) to support TPEM’s change content requirements for the first vehicle development.
JLR's EMA platform will underpin JLR's next generation of 'pure electric' mid-sized SUVs for international markets, to be launched from 2025 onwards.

This EMA architecture will be used for its upcoming premium range of EVs based on the Avinya concept showcased by the company, said P B Balaji, Group CFO of Tata Motors.

The access to JLR's EMA platform will help accelerate TPEM's entry into the high end EV segment, while reducing development cycle time and costs. JLR's advanced E&E architecture is capable of going to L2+ autonomy with all advanced connectivity, comfort and convenience features.

Further, it will accelerate TPEM's adoption of Software over the Air (SOTA), Features over the Air (FOTA), safety (5 Star Euro NCAP Rating) and ultra fast charging technologies for high performance vehicles.

VinFast is looking for sites for it's EV Manufacturing facility in India, visits Tamil Nadu.
01 Nov 2023 1:45 P.M. IST

Vietnamese electric vehicle (EV) maker VinFast Auto is looking for sites to set up a manufacturing facility in India and has visited two locations in Tamil Nadu, a report said. Vinfast, which started making EVs in the year 2021, have visited sites in the Manalur region, north of Chennai and also in the state’s southern-most district of Tuticorin.
The company has expressed its interest to establish a manufacturing facility in India with an estimated investment of around $200 million to start assembling vehicles in India by the year 2026 to what it pronunces as a market with tremendous potential. VinFast unveiled it's plans during its quarter two results announcement on September 24, for its next phase of development to begin in 2024. One of it's important strategies is the expansion of it's dealership network into new markets, with a special focus on entering the Indian market.
Managing director and global CEO of VinFast Auto Mr. Le Thi Thu Thuy said on October 5, “We are looking at a total capacity up to 50,000 cars (per year) in each facility in Phase I with an estimated total coverage of $150-200 million. We expect to participate in the tremendous potential brought by increased EV adoption in both Indonesia and India where EV penetration is currently still very low,” he emphasized.
Vietnamese company founded in 2017 has ambitious plans to take on Elon Musk's Tesla in the United States, where Tesla has a market share of 50 percent.
